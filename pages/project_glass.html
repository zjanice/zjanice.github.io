HowTo

A Google Glass application visually helps people learn how to finish a task

Background

HowTo is a Google Glass application aimed at helping people with executive function impairment, but could also be used in a general context to teach a difficult task. The basic idea is to break down a complex task into simple chunks and make an interactive tutorial with step by step instructions and visual aid. â€‹

Our target user include:
- Children with learning disabilities who need help with their home work
- Older adults with cognitive or memory deficits
- Anyone who wants to learn something in a novel, interactive way

1. How does the system work?

1. Break down a complex task into simple chunks and make an interactive tutorial with step by step instructions and visual aid (images).

2. Provide two-way communication:

Learner who is watching the tutorial may send short messages to Helper;
Helper may choose to send back messages or initiate video call

Building Up

Based on the user research and discussion, I created wireframes and prototype for three iterations. Each iteration was followed by a kickoff meeting.
Tools: index cards, pen and pencils, ticky notes, marker pens, transparent plastic sheet.
