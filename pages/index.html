About

I am a Ph.D. student studying Human-Centered Computing at Georgia Institute of Technology, under the advisory of Dr. Andrea Grimes Parker.

My research interest lies at the intersection of crisis informatics, visualization, and health sciences. My research broadly contributes to the fields of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Computer Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), Information Visualization (VIS), and Health Informatics.

My PhD dissertation examines how people interact with and perceive the massive amounts of evolving and emergent information and technologies during crises, with a focus on marginalized communities. I further examine how trust forms, shifts, and collapses as crisis information infrastructures evolve. To explore how technological systems can be designed to reduce inequity, I capture people’s interactions with technologies and the dynamics of people’s perceptions during emerging crises through surveys, interviews, observations, co-design, systematic analysis of artifacts, and user evaluations. My research sheds light on how technologies can mitigate informational vulnerability and help marginalized groups navigate through crises.
