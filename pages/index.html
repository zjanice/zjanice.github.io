About

I am a Ph.D. candidate studying Human-Centered Computing at Georgia Institute of Technology, under the advisory of Dr. Andrea Grimes Parker.

I am currently on the academic job market this cycle, looking for a tenure-track assistant professor position beginning fall 2023.

My research broadly lies in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Data Visualization (VIS), Equity, and cross-disciplines of crisis informatics and health informatics. My work has been well recognized by academia: I was named a Rising Star in EECS 2022, and selected as a Foley Scholar (the highest award for student excellence in research contributions to computing at Georgia Tech). The impact of my research extends beyond academia: I was invited to present my work focused on crisis visualization design and public trust to broader audiences such as the World Health Organization (WHO).

My PhD dissertation has focused on examining how people form their online trust in a way that promotes the usage of information and technologies, and how we can design trustworthy information and technologies that enhance wellbeing, with a focus on vulnerable and marginalized populations. To answer these questions, I have used a variety of empirical methods, such as (longitudinal) surveys, interviews, observations, co-design, systematic analysis of artifacts, and user evaluations.

In my spare time, I am a photographer . I also have a sweet puppy---a Border Collie Dog breed (and his name is "Sunny") . Some of my hobbies teach me how to see the world and research from other lens through my camera and my puppy's perspective, with love and peace.

Active Projects

Crisis Informatics
From information consumers' perspectives: Examine information behaviors and online trust among marginalized communities amidst COVID-19
Overview: In this work, we conducted mixed-methods, longitudinal research to examine how people’s trust and distrust emerge, transform, and collapse in the processes of information seeking amidst the COVID-19 pandemic.
From information producers' perspectives: Investigate crisis visualization production and decision-making processes and its' impact on the public
Overview: This thread of work focuses on crisis visualizations (i.e., visual representations of information about COVID-19)---an emerging but integral and critical component of information infrastructure. We examined who, (uses) what data, (to communicate) what messages, in what form, under what circumstances in the context of COVID-19 crisis visualizations, to whom, and with what effect.

Visualization in Decision-making & Sensemaking
Exploring how visualization can be designed to support sensemaking and decision making in different domains (e.g., chronic illness like diabetes, collobrative visualizations).
