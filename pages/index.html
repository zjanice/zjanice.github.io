About

I am a Ph.D. student studying Human-Centered Computing at Georgia Institute of Technology, under the advisory of Dr. Andrea Grimes Parker. Prior to transferring to Georgia Tech, I was a Ph.D. student in Personal Health Informatics at Northeastern University.

My research interest lies at the intersection of visualization, health, and crisis informatics. I am broadly interested in studying how people perceive and use information and communication technologies (ICTs), with a focus on examining equity issues in technology design and usage, and vulnerable populations. I see vulnerability a relational and relative concept that can increase due to physical and psychological constraints (e.g., older adults suffering from mobility, sensory, and cognitive limitations) and socio-economic disruptions (e.g., crisis situations), rather than a static characteristic of an individual or a particular group. To guide my empirical and design work, I capture human experiences, perceptions, and views through a variety of methods, such as interviews, observations, participatory design, surveys, and systematic analysis of artifacts. Below, I discuss how I have contributed to disciplinary knowledge and critical conversations about how vulnerable populations perceive and use technologies, and how technologies can be designed to help them address their needs.
My research contributes to the fields of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Computer Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), Information Visualization (VIS), and Health Informatics.
