Fall
CSCI 420/520. Intro to Human-Computer Interaction
Overview: This course introduces the basics of human-computer interaction design and evaluation methods and techniques, such as examining user tasks, developing scenarios and storyboards, creating prototypes, iterating on prototypes to create a final design project, and evaluating the prototype. Other topics may include computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW), ethics, and human-AI-interaction.
Spring
CSCI 780. Human-Centered Computing
Overview: This class introduces students to theoretical readings and foundations in Human-Centered Computing. Topics covered may include an introduction to a wide range of theories and their application to the design of interactive computing systems, such as sociology of science and technology, situated action, participatory design, and feminism HCI.
